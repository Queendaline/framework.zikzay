CHAPTER 10

WATER

Water is one of the most common substances. It is a substance which can exist in the three state of matter, as solid, (ice), liquid, (water) and gaseous water or steam (vapor). It is a universal solvent in that it can dissolve a wide range of substance.

SOURCES OF WATER

Water occurs rarely in its pure form in nature. It can be obtained as natural water and as treated water.

NATURAL SOURCES OF WATER

Natural water includes rain water, spring water, lake water, river, well water, sea water etc.

  1. RAIN WATER: - It is the purest form of natural water because it is formed as a result of condensation of water vapor in the atmosphere. It is a natural form of distilled water.
  2. SPRING WATER: - It is a good source of drinking water. It contains a considerable amount of mineral salt but very little suspended impurities like dust and bacteria.
  3. WELL WATER: - We have deep well and surface well. Well water contains a lot of clay and other mineral salts. Water from deep well (like borehole) are less polluted than that from surface well.
  4. SEA WATER: - The sea is the chief source of water. It is hard and contains a lot of dissolved salts, dissolved air, bacteria, organic remains etc
  5. RIVER WATER; - It contains a lot of dissolved air, mineral salt, bacterial, organic remains. Rivers are used extensively for sewage disposal.

 

TREATED SOURCE OF WATER

It includes (i) distilled water (ii) pipe borne water and (iii) chlorinated water

Treated water is usually prepared for special purpose; Pipe borne water for lawn water supply. Chlorinated water for use in swimming pool.

Distilled water is chemical pure water. It is prepared by condensing stem or passing water through anion exchange resin column.

USES OF DISTILLED WATER
  1. It is used in the lab for preparing reagents and analytical work.
  2. It is used for certain industrial purpose
  3. It is used for the preparation of drugs
  4. It is used in car batteries

PHYSICAL PROPERTIES OF WATER

  1. Pure water is a clear, colorless, tasteless and odourless liquid.
  2. It has a melting point of 0oC (273K) and a boiling point of 100oC (373K) at atmosphere of 760mmHg
  3. It has a maximum density of 1g at 4oC
  4. Pure water is poor conductor of electricity

ADVANTAGES OF HARDWATER
  1. Calcium salt in water helps in formation of strong and healthy teeth and bones.
  2. It helps animals such as Snails and Crabs to make their shells. (made of CaCO3).
  3. It does not dissolve lead, hence can be supplied in land pipe.
  4. Hard water has a nice taste and its preferred in brewing beer.
DISADVANTAGES OF HARDWATER
  1. It leads to wastage of soap in laundering due to formation of scum.
  2. It helps to generate inefficiency of industrial boilers.
  3. It gives white material dull appearance in washing with it.
  4. It interferes with dyeing and taming processes.
  5. It can lead to Lead poisoning

WATER POLLUTION

Water pollution is the introduction of unwanted materials into water bodies. It occurs when harmful substances enter water bodies (like rivers, streams etc)

CAUSES WATER POLLUTANTS

The main cause of water pollution is the indiscriminate dumping of solids and liquid wastes into water bodies. Some water pollutants include;

  1. Industrial Wastes (Eg Acids, Detergents, Organic Solvents, alkali mercury compounds, In sea trades) etc.
  2. Agricultural wastes eg Fertilizers
  3. Untreated sewage (eg feces, waste water from home and industries).
  4. Refuse/liters eg. Paper, plastics, cans, food, etc.
  5. Crude oil spills
  6. Thermal pollution, water used for cooling. In same industries refinery, brewery is pumped back into the river, thereby causing the death of some aquatic organism like fishes.
CONTROL OF WATER POLLUTION
  1. Sewage should be processed, treated and converted to useful fertilizers in sewage plants.
  2. Refuse should be burned or burnt in an incubator with in-built devices to prevent air pollution.
  3. Chemical waste should be converted to harmless biodegradable substance before being dumped into the sea.
  4. Safety measures must be implemented to prevent crude oil spills.
  5. Strict laws must be passed to control water pollution by water individuals and companies.
  6. Educating people should also serve as anti-pollution measure to make people aware of the dangers of water pollution.

USES OF WATER

Water is a universal solvent; in that it dissolves a wide variety of substances (inorganic and organic) to some extent to form aqueous solution. Many of its uses depend in its ability to dissolve many different types of substances.

  1. FOR LIFE PURPOSES: - Water is needed for life purposes like photosynthesis. Each cell in a living organism needs water to function properly. Aquatic organisms depend on dissolved oxygen in water for respiration.
  2. FOR DOMESTIC USES: - Water is needed for drinking, cooking, washing and cleaning in the home.
  3. FOR INDUSTRIAL USES: - water is used in food and chemical industries as raw material in the production of goods, paper, metals and chemicals like drug. It is used for cooking and cleaning processes in industries.
  4. FRO RECREATIONAL PURPOSES: - Lake sides, beaches, and water parks are some places for people to carry out leisure activities such as sailing, swimming, etc. it is needed for building and maintaining swimming pools.
  5. IN AGRICULTURAL: - Water is used for irrigation of farmlands and for fish ponds etc.

 

LABORATORY PREPARATION OF DISTILLED (DEIONIZED) WATER

APPARATUS USED: - Distillation apparatus (distillation flask) thermometer, condenser, Bunsen burner, Tripod stand, wire gauge, beakers.

MATERIAL USED: - Salt solution, boiling chips tap water.

PROCEDURE: -

  1. Pour some salt solution into the distillation flask.
  2. Heat the solution in the distillation flask to vaporize the water.
  3. The vapor is passed along the condenser and cooled by circulating water in its Southen Jacket
  4. The vapor condensed into liquid water is collected in the receiver (beaker) as distillated water.

Distilled water is obtained in the laboratory by simple distillation.

PURIFICATION OF WATER

Water can be purified by the following methods.

  1. Coagulation (flocculation)
  2. Sedimentation
  3. Filtration
  4. Disinfection (eg chlorination)
