BASICITY OF ACIDS

The basicity of an acid is the number of replaceable hydrogens in one molecule of the acid. Acids can be classified as;

  1. MONOBASIC ACID:- A monobasic is the acid containing one replace hydrogen ion H+ in one molecule of the acid, Eg. HCl, CH3­COOH, H
  2. A DIBASIC ACID:- is the acid which contains two replaceable hydrogen ion H+ in one molecule of the acid, eg, H2SO4, H2CO3, H2SO4.
  3. A TRIBASIC ACID:- is the acid which contains three replaceable hydrogen ion H+, in one molecule of acid, eg, H3SO­4

  1. Dilute acids have sour taste eg, unripe fruits, vinegar, and rancid milk.

  3. Acids are colourless, odourless liquids.

BASES

A base is a substance which neutralizes an acid to form salt and water only, this is called neutralization. Most oxides and hydroxides of metals are bases. Some metallic(basic) oxides are Sodium oxide (NaO), potassium oxide (K2O), calcium oxides (CaO), Aluminum oxide (Al2­­O);

Some basic hydroxide include: - Calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)2) Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) etc, most bases are insolable in water eg Fe2O­3, ZnO, Cu(OH)2, Al2O3, Mg(OH)3 etc. Na2CO3 is also a base.

ALKALI: - An alkaline is a basic hydroxide which is soluble in water, eg NaOH, KOH, NH­3­H2O.

An alkali is  a compound which produce hydroxide ion OH, when dissolves in water. Bases can be obtained locally from wood ash (by burning dry palm fruit)

RELATIVE ACIDITY AND ALKALINITY

Indicator are weak organic acids or bases. They are used to indicate the acidity or alkalinity of a solution. Many indicators are colour-extracts from plants, other examples include; methyl orange, phenolphthalein, methyl red etc.

The indicators like litmus paper or solution can tell us whether a solution is acidic or available but cannot tell us the degree of acidity or a alkalinity of the solution.

pH SCALE:- PH is a measure of the degree of acidity or alkalinity of a solution. pH is referred to as the hydrogen ion concentration (H+) or hydroxide ion concentration (OH)- of a solution. The pH scale is set of numbers ranging from 0 to 14.

Acids have pH values less than 7

Alkalis have pH value greater than 7

Neutral solutions have pH value of 7

pH indicators are compounds that can exhibit different colors in acidic and alkaline solution.

 

UNIVERSAL INDICATOR

A universal indicator is mixture of various indicators which work at different ph environments with values of about 3 to 11. It changes its color over wide range, from red through orange, yellow, green, blue, violet to purple in accordance with the pH of the medium with which it is in contact.

IMPORTANCE OF PH

  1. PH is used in medicine and pharmacy. The PH of body fluids like blood, urine and gastric juice provides valuable diagnostic information to a physician. PH of a normal human blood is about 7-4.

  4. The regulation of PH is important in sewage disposal treatment of industrial waste and environment protection of rivers.

 

SALTS

A salt is the compound formed when all parts of the ionization hydrogen of acid is replaced by a metallic or ammonium ion. It is a compound containing an aggregate positive metallic or ammonium ions and negative acid radical ion.

 

TYPES OF SALTS.

There are four main types of salts

  1. NORMAL SALT: A normal salt is the salt in which the hydrogen ions in an acid are completely replaced by metallic ions or ammonium ions.

E.g. Sodium chloride, NaCl, ZnSO4, Na2CO3

This HCl + NaOH  NaCl + H2O

       acid                  normal salt.

Normal salts are neutral to litmus.

  1. ACID SALTS:- is the one in which the hydrogen ions in an acid is partially replaced by a metallic ion.

E.g NaHCO3, NaHSO4, KHCO3, Ca(HCO3)2  Thus

         H2SO4 + NaOH  NaHSO4 + H2O

         Acid salts turns blue litmus red.

  1. BASIC SALTS;- is the one in which insufficient acid is used to neutralize the base. E.g Mg(OH)Cl, Ba(OH)Cl, Zn(OH)Cl,

              Zn(OH)Cl + HCl  ZnCl2 + H2O 

               It turns red litmus blue.

  1. DOUBLE SALT: - is one which ionizes to form three different types of ions. In solution usually two are positively charged (metallic or ammonium ions) while the other is negatively charged ion eg (NHo4­)2SO4, FeSO4.6H2O, KCl, MgCl2.6H2O ie (NH4)2, FeSO4.3H2O,
  2. COMPLEX SALTS:- is the salt which contains complex ion consisting of changed groups of atoms.

 

METHODS OF PREPARATION OF SALTS

The method chosen for the preparation of a particular salt depend on: -

Its solubility in water

Its solubility to heat.

      Soluble salts are prepared by methods involving crystallization while insoluble salts are prepared by methods of involving precipitation.

 

METHODS OF PREPARATION OF SOLUBLE SALTS.

Soluble salts can be prepared by the following methods

Neutralization of a base(alkali) by an acid

          NaOH + H2SO4  Na2SO4 + H2O

Action of dilute acid in an insoluble base

          CuO + H2SO4  CuSO4 + H2O

Action of dilute acid on a trioxocarbonate(iv)

CuCO3 + H2SO4  CuSO4 + H2O + CO2

Action of dilute acid on metal

          Zn + 2HCl  ZnCl2 + H2                   soluble salt

  2. 2Fe(s) + 3Cl2(g) 2FeCl3(s)                           Insoluble salt

ACID, BASE REACTION (TITRATION)

To Titrate Sodium Hydroxide against dilute Hydrochloric acid

APPARATUS: - Burette (50cm3), Pipette (250cm3), Conical Flasks, beakers, funnel etc.

MATERIAL: - dilute hydrochloric acid, Sodium hydroxide, methyl orange indicators.

PROCEDURE: - 25cm3 of dilute NaOH solution is pipetted into the conical flask and two drops of methyl orange indicator is added to dilute HCl acid from the burette, is added little by little until there is a color change.

Then the solution in the conical flask is now neutralized and this is called the end point of the reaction, the volume of the acid used is recorded. Thus experiment is repeated for about three times and the arranged titration (titre) is calculated.
