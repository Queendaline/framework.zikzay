Unit 1

 Pre-colonial or Folk Traditional Dances

  1. Pre-colonial — This means before the advent or coming of the white man or our former colonial masters.
  2. Pre-colonial Dance — This means dance patterns that existed before the coming of our colonial masters.
  3. Folk is referring to people i.e. the ordinary people who live in a particular area especially a traditional setting.
  4. Tradition — Belief, or way of doing some things which has existed for a long time. Take for instance the Ibos have a tradition of hard work and Self-sufficiency.
  5. Traditional — Being part of the traditions of a people e.g. traditional Hausa soft drink — Zobo. Pre-colonial or Folk Traditional are Dances that existed in a particular area and handed down from generation to generation before the coming of the white man and his colonization. The Nigerian nation is peopled by people who are creative and vibrant in nature. For example, the Mbaise people of Imo State, Afikpo people of Ebonyi State, Birom people of Plateau State, the Fulani of the North, the Hausas of the North, Fidiwo people of the Egbaland of Ogun State, the ljebus of Ogun State, the ljaws of Rivers, Bayelsa and Delta State etc, to mention but a few. These peoples have their traditional folk dances for leisure and ceremonies.

 

Note: When a dance has been handed down from many generations, it could be called traditional folk dance for example.

  • Atilogwu of the Igbos
  • Mkpokiti of the Igbos
  • Bata of the Yorubas
  • Adigigbo of the Yorubas
  • Sharo of the Fulanis
  • Langa of the Hausas

This is to mention but a few.

 

  1. Atilogwu Dance:

Atilogwu dance originated from Afikpo — Enugu axis and spread to other parts of Igboland. It is regarded as the national dance of the Igbo people of the South East of Nigeria. The dance is characterized by acrobatic display, vigorous shaking of the body and gestures that make spectators laugh and feel happy. It requires a lot of speed, energy and vigor. Atilogwu, because of the nature, is a dance of the youth. It is only the youth that can meet the requirements of the dance — fexbiliy, agility, coordination speed. nation and speed.

 Equipment: Drums, big gong, udu, oyo, the costume (attire) bright and colourful.

 

  1. Bata Dance: This is a Yoruba cultural dance. It is displayed or performed in honour of the Sango god, the thunder deity which is very much respected honoured and feared among the Yoruba traditionalists. The bata drum from where it derived its name is the most prominent instrument. Dungy the dance, some bata cultists under the frenzy display some magical acts Families dedicated to the Sango diety (called bata family) publicly danoa b renew their covenant with the bat Cultism.

 

  1. Agidigbo Dance

This is another Yoruba traditional dance associated with the wise. Old men who have the ancestral wisdom decare the minds of the gods in proverbial and idiomatic expressions allowing only the wise to go home with the message. The dance performance is carried out by - energetic young men who dance to the weird, strong and fast beats of the weird drums.

  1. Sharo Dance

 This originates from the Fulani in Nigeria. It is a dance performed in the flogging competition of young men wishing to marry i.e. passing through traditional rite of passage especially for Jafun, Fulani men. During the drumming, suitors are flogged stoically and are expected to endure the lashes for show their manhood and willingness to marry. It is usually done during Id-el-Kabir festival.

 

Unit 2

Social Dances

Social dance: Is a dance performance done on a platform of balloon dance during social gatherings or convivialities. Often it is performed by opposite sexes holding hands, rolling and rocking or clinging together It is a dance meant to give relaxation to socialites after a day’s hard work. It is purely for leisure and pleasure and not for public entertainment. This Category of dance, there are no judges, no standards set. Most often the music is on a soft tire and Classical. Some of these socialites or dancers still hold their drinking cups while dancing or even charting with their dance partners while still dancing. This form of dance is easily seen among people of high social reckoning i.e the upper class comprising the top civil servants, industrialists, top military officers and to flight merchants.

Foreign and Modern Dances

 

There are few foreign but modem dances that have permeated the Nigerian dance industry.

Some of these include;

  1. Raggae Dance: This dance originates from Jamaica it was however popularized by Bob Marley and Peter Tosh. The dancers either plait their hairs or wear long twisted hair called dada. It follows a somewhat systematic and regular staggering steps and movements giving the impression that the dancers are possessed by extraneous power. The dancers: Steps match with the beats as they move back and forth. They are Called rastafarians. In Nigeria we had Majek Fashek, Ras Kimono, The Prophets, etc.

 

  1. Disco dance: This form of dance Originated from western Europe particularly from Britain and America(U.S.A.). it is a dance which demands energy, dancing to the rhythm of the beats using one’s legs, arms shoulder head and waist. Often, one could jumpdance-break, engage in fast meant for the energetic youths and are purely performed to entertain the public. Micheal Jackson popularised this dance and music.
  1. High life dance: This dance originated from the upper class in Ghana soon after Ghana's
  2. independence in 1957. Kt is an easy-going dance which demands slow but rhythmic movements or twisting of one’s body and, or waist. It is a scaled down traditional energy sapping dance by the youths. The high life dance is mostly performed by people beyond forty years who need graceful steps to show their age, wealth, status and modernity in life style. It came to Nigeria in the early 1960"s. star high life musicians in Nigeria include Sir Dr. Warrior, Oliver De Coque, Chief Osita Osadebe, Victor Olaiya, Sunny Ade, Ebenezar Obey, Sir Morrocco Kabaka, Sir Victor Uwaifo.
  3. Rumba dance: This is a dance which is usually slow as it follows the songs of love and passion stories between a man and a woman. it appears romantic, people lovers, clamping together to demonstrate emotion and burning passion for one another. It does not demand energetic turns, steps and movements but slow graceful, and passionate movements that soothes the nerves.
  4. Classical dance: Dancers of this music, dance, to, the classic. tune rhyme and rhythm of the music. The music originating from Europe and popularized in Britain in the Middle Ages, is often called a ballet dance. This dance as used to tell story in dramatic Performance with sounds (music) not accompanied with songs of speech. The dramatic steps of movements agreeing with the musical instruments dramatically tell the story. In some cases, it Is led by a ballerina also known as prima ballerina i.e a female dancer in a ballet who leads or takes active, part in the dance. This dance is also used as a concert to entertain the public particularly the upper class.
