Unit 1

Meaning and Types of Family

The word family comes from a Latin word familia. It means a group of people who’s living together is made possible by birth or co-residence. They share their money, food, drinks sometimes expenses and thought. In most cases, a typical Nigerian family goes by a common surname. A family is also seen as the smallest of the society and the institution for socialization of children.

 

Family Types

There are three (3) main types of family

  1. The nuclear Family or immediate family. This is made up of father, mother and children and in the case of childlessness, husband and wife.
  2. The extended family is made up of grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, nephews, nieces and of course, the immediate parents.
  3. Single Parent Family: This is made up of either a parent-father or parent-mother and children.
  4. However, there are other family variations.
  5. Polygamous family: In this case the husband has two or more wives.
  6. Step family: This is created as of death, divorce or separation. It involves two separate family or unit. It consists of a foster father or mother.
  7. Grand parent family: in this case, grandparents are saddled with the responsibility of raising their grandchildren due to the death of their direct parent.

Puberty in Boys and Girls

What is Puberty?

 Puberty could be seen as the stage of physical development during which one can change from child to adult and is able to have children. It could also be seen as the period during which growing boys and girls undergo the process of maturation. This is evident in the development of some physical secondary sex characteristics such as pubic hairs, rapid growth, etc. The onset of puberty differs from one individual to the other. It differs from boys to girls. For example, the time Jane starts experiencing puberty may not be the time Mercy will experience hers yet they were born the same month. Again, puberty in girls occurs before that of the boys. Usually, Puberty in girls occurs between the ages 10 years to 14 years while that of the boys occurs between 12 years to 16 years. However, the above figures can no longer be relied on due to improvements on nutrition, environment, and genetic factors.

 

Major Features of Puberty

Assertiveness and Communication Skills

Definition of Terms

Assertive — This is saying or doing something with all amount of assurance before people. Assertiveness is not being aggressive rather, it is one is asking for his own while considering others people’s rights, wants, and needs. In assertiveness, one considers the rights and privileges of the other person and by so doing, finds a balance. It is not always easy to identify assertive behavior. This is because there is a thin line between assertiveness and aggressiveness. Assertive people act to always achieve fair play and resist intimidation. When one treats others with fairness and respect, he or she gets that same treatment in return and people would want to work with him/her. It is unfortunate in our society today very few people are assertive.

Assertiveness is important in our homes, society, the state, in the church for a better society. Some People are naturally more assertive towards being aggressive, it is important you work on the following

  • Understand that your rights, thoughts, feelings, needs, and desires are just as important as everyone else's.
  • Realize that they are not more important than anyone else’s either.
  • Recognize your right and go for it reasonably.
  • Don’t always put yourself in situation you apologize all the time.
  • Allow yourself to be angry but be rights, wants, and needs. in respect
  • Control yourself no matter the situation
  • Learn to accept criticisms
  • Know that you can’t do everything or please everyone in everything for advice and direction.
  • It has been observed that engaging in sports activities such as football, handball, volley ball, playing care watching T.V, video games dancing, writing listening to music or hanging out with friends help a lot to allay one’s worries and anger.

Communication simply put, is the process by which people exchange information or express their thoughts, ideas and feelings. Communication is Vital in every aspect of life, be it in the home, church, organization, gymnasium classroom, etc.

It has been observed that most people particularly the young ones cannot communicate assertively without being aggressive. And as leaders of tomorrow it is pertinent that you learn to win the hearts of your listeners when addressing them. Assertive communication technique is essential. To convey basic assertions, it is good to use “! want”, “I need” or “I feel’. For example, Jane would rather suggest you keep this pregnancy to avoid exposing you to danger.

If Jane insists, do not flay up rather change your verb. Jane, my daughter, have you forgotten that disobedience is not allowed in our family. Unit 6

Teenage Pregnancy and Its consequences

The word teenage refers to those individuals from the age of thirteen (13) to nineteen (19) years old. Sometimes they are referred to as teens. Teenage pregnancy or teen pregnancy refers to pregnancy contracted during the above age bracket i.e. 13 — 19 years. Teenage pregnancy could result from marriage between a man and teenage wife or between a boy and his teenage girlfriend who were not well tutored during their puberty years. Sometimes a teenager may decide to get pregnant due to youthful exuberance. Whichever way teenage pregnancy is not the best because of the risks associated with it.

 

The consequences of teenage Pregnancy are numerous. These include:

  • The girl’s education might be stalled if she is not from a rich home.
  • It socially stigmatizes a girl leading to most of her friends abandoning her because she is now regarded as a mother who will take care of her baby while her peers are in school.
  • There is also financial pressure on the family which may lead to poverty and malnutrition.
  • Teenage may resort to drinking, drug abuse, and sometimes stealing.
  • In most cultures and particularly in Africa, the child is branded a bastard and does not enjoy any family inheritance.
  • Sometimes, mother and child are malnourished due to lack of good and balanced diet.
  • A teenager may suffer an emotional crisis if she becomes pregnant and does not want the baby, this may lead to rash behaviour such as trying to abort the baby and suicide attempt.

Career Guidance

  • Career: The word career means a job or profession that you have been trained for and which you do for a long period of your life.
  • Guidance: This means help and advice that is given to someone about their work, education or personal life.
  • Career Guidance: Career Guidance consists of the services that help people successfully develop and manage their career development. The people involved in providing these services are referred to as career guidance providers. They are found in schools, hospitals, ministries, industries, universities, and other higher institutions, etc to assist people in their choice of career. In secondary schools they are called guidance counselors / Officers. There may be two or more of them depending on the numerical strength of the school. The counselors are there in schools, to assist the students.
