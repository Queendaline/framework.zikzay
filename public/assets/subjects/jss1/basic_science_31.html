Different parts of the body have different points they attract to the competitor provided the permitted techniques are used. For instance:

Note! One-point deduction will be given for:

  • Attacking an opponent after Kal-yeo.
  • Attacking a fallen opponent.
  • Intentionally attacking the Opponent's face with the hand.

 

Note again! ½ point deductions will be affected when an:

  • Attacker uses his knee on the opponent
  • Attacks the opponent below the waist.
  • Evading an attack by turning his back to the opponent.
  • Pretending to have been injured.
  • Avoiding a match.
  • Grabbing or pushing an opponent.

Punch 

Boxing is all about punching and king points. There are four basic punches in boxing. They include the following:

  • Uppercut
  • Jab
  • Hook

 

Uppercut

This is vertical rising punch taken from below the with level of the opponent's chest the knees slightly bent. It is usually a devastation punch if executed.

 

 

 Cross

This is a powerful, straight punch on the chin. There are two

 

types: The right cross and the left cross for the left-handed or south paw. The cross punch pierces any line of defence to its target-the chin.

 

Jab

Jab is short quick, straight punch on its target. After making contact with the target, the lead hand is retracted (withdrawn) quicky to resume a guard in front of the face.

 

 Hook

 The hook is a semicircular punch directed to the side of the opponent's head. Sometimes a hook may target the lower part of the body, this technique is sometimes called the ‘rip’. Mike Tyson used this technique often to demolish his opponents.

 

Attack

It is a forward movement into an opponent in order to score points using any of the punching techniques. In any successful attack an opponent is not given a chance to retaliate.

 

Dodging

Dodging simply means moving away from danger, so in bonding, it is moving away from the opponent’s punches. This could take the form of bending low to avert a possible jab or hook from an opponent or stepping back with the head tilted backward, to avoid across or an uppercut.

 Defence

This is an act of protecting someone from attack. This could take the form of stepping backwards or covering the vita! parts of the head-eye, ear, nose, etc. from being hit by the opponent.
