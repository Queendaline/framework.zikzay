Effects of Drugs Abuse on Youth

The main effects of drug abuse on youths are as follows:

  1. It may lead to physical injuries and loss of lives due to motor car and motorcycle accidents.
  2. People who are involved in alcohol and illicit drugs have increased risks of death through homicidal suicide, illness and accident.
  3. It may lead to psychological damage such as mental disorders like madness, anxiety, depression, restlessness, hallucination, apathy, sleeplessness, psychosis and so on.
  4. The use of marijuana could cause short term memory learning effect psychomotor skills.
  5. you may notice certain changes in the behaviour such as withdrawal from the family, resentful attitude and disruptive behaviour with people, poor academic performances, truancy in school and workplace, hatred for handwork, loss of sense of judgement etc.
  6. Such youths engage in behaviours that place them at a high risk of HIV infection e.g rape, prostitution, divorce etc.
  7. It may result to drug addiction and violent behaviours such as murder, armed robbery, fighting, and vandalism.

 

Effects of Drugs Abuse on the Family
  • It can cause marital problems such as wife battering, divorce and family disintegration etc.
  • It may result to poor work perform and loss of job (due to unethical work behaviour) which seriously affect the family.
  • Pregnant mothers are very prone to contracting HIV/AIDS through the use of infected injection needle.

 

Effects of Drug Abuse on the Society
  • The people involved may lose their jobs and become public nuisance.
  • Such people may disturb the neighborhood especially during the time by fighting other violent behaviours.
  • Many deaths may occur accidents involving consumption alcohols and other hard drugs.

The Role and Activities of National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC)

NAFDAC 1s a parastatal of the Federal Ministry of Health established by decree No. 15 of 1993 NAFDAC has Vanous Basic Functions According to the requirements of its enabling decree. The agency was authorized.

  1. Regulate and control the importation, exportation, manufacture, advertisement, distribution, sale, and use of drugs, cosmetics, medical devices, bottled water and chemicals.
  2. Conduct appropriate tests and ensure compliance with standard specifications ~ designated and approved by the council for the effective control of quantity of drugs, food, cosmetics, medical devices, bottled water and chemicals.
  3. Undertake appropriate investigation into the production premises and raw materials for food, cosmetics, medical devices, bottled water and chemicals and establish relevant quality assurance System, including certification of the production sites and of the regulated products.
  4. Advises federal, state and local governments, the private sector and other interested bodies regarding the quality, safety ‘and regulatory provision on food, drug, cosmetics medical devices, bottled water and chemical.
  5. undertake inspection of imported foods, drugs cosmetics, assurance system including certification of production sites and of the regulated products.
