Unit 1

Meaning of Skill and Skill Acquisition

 

Skill is learned and acquired ability to perform a service or an economic activity capable of transforming the Society, aid in economic growth and development. it is also capable of placing an individual in a vantage position where he or she acquires integrity, respectability, socioeconomic elevation and self-reliance. In other words, skill is display of inherited and, or acquired abilities or potentials which make a person socioeconomically useful to the wellbeing, social and economic development of his immediate or international community.

 

Possession of skills distinguishes ma from other creatures. Skill creates work or labour and essentially creates sense and levels of worth in man.

 

Generally, all men are born with elements of rudimentary or basic skills which are referred to as talents. These natural talents on their own are libidos or propensity to perform tasks or jobs amateurly without training. These skills can be enhanced through coordinated and effective training or would hope to achieve economic and education. Man being a homofaber (animal that works) can only realize his purpose through task performance.

 
Meaning of Skill Acquisition

skill acquisition is the perfection or transformation of one’s potentials or the learning of additional skills which one intends to use for economic or social reward. It is acquired through conscious training and or internship for maximum productivity and benefits.

Unit 3

Reasons for Skill Acquisition

 

A skill’ is not acquired without a purpose. There are many reasons why skills are acquired and practiced. 

 

 

 

These include:

  1. Skills are Acquired to Deal with Risky or Complex Activities: Certain resources may not be tapped or made available for use due to the risks associated with their availability. For example, electricity which is needed to run our homes, industries, farms, businesses and offices is shrouded with great amount of risks that only those who are specially and specifically trained can afford to meddle with it for the supply of electrical energy. An amateur can easily be electrocuted or manipulate it to cause havoc to systems, properties and lives. A fishing ang fisherman has to acquire Swimming skills as fishing on high Sea is enormously risky. understand the complexities of certain objects, it becomes proper that the skills associated with them be learned and acquired for proper exploitation and management of the resources.
  2. Skill acquisition helps us to take decision: Decision making is needed as one desires which skill to acquire. At acquiring the skill, he decides to use it for profiteering or for philanthropy. Skill acquisition could lead to taking a decision on what to embark on, what to produce and how to go about it.

 

  1. Managing emergency situation could be another reason for embarking on skill acquisition: Fire fighters and military strategists are particularly trained to contain emergency situations in terms of massive and sudden conflagration or security breakdown and insurgency. Medical doctors and nurses are also trained to handle outbreaks of pandemic diseases such as avian flu, swine flu, HIV/AIDS and most recently, the Ebola Virus Disease (EVD). Acquiring such professional skills could help in curbing such menace.

 

  1. Survival strategy is yet another reason for skill acquisition: Basically, the essence learning a skill is to achieve economic self-sufficiency. A person without a Skill loses the chance to survive succeed socially and economically with a skill one can be engaged to eke-out a living. Skill acquisition is a strategy and means to life. The essence of employing or engaging a worker is to make him practice his skill for which he is rewarded. The more trained or skilled a person is, the more he is sought after and rewarded Based on this, workers can be Classified as nonskilled, semi-skilled and skilled.

 

  1. An Individual with skills believes he is useful to the society and feels a sense of responsibility, a sense of duty to contribute to the development of the society .

 

  1. Another reason for skill acquisition is to help the learner acquire useful skills which will make him useful, intelligent producer and consumer in the society.

 

  1. Due to personal prestige and respect: An individual can acquire skill. It is obvious that an idle person or unskilled individual is regarded as a failure, poor and of low class. This has a lot of implications on the person's level, of socialization, socio-economic opportunities and political relevance. in order to avoid being consigned to this level of oblivion, individuals struggle to acquire skills to avoid idleness, self-inflicted poverty and earn public prestige, dignity and respect.

 

  1. The urge to fulfil God's injunction that people should Labour, makes people acquire Skills: At creation, man was made a co-labourer with God and when man sinned, he was made to work harder (Gen. 3:17). The scripture challenged - Christians to work if they must eat (II Thess. 3:10). These have made people to learn skills in order to fit into the plan of God. In order to share in the joy of being part of production in the process of division of labour, people endeavour to acquire some ‘Skills needed in such that production might take place.

 

  1. Some engage in skills acquisition in order to domicile the Skill in the family, making it part of the culture and heritage: In like manner, some individuals learn skills in order to sustain or take charge of an established family business for perpetuity so as not to lose their accruing benefits.

 

Unit 4

Importance of Skill Acquisition

The importance of skill acquisition cannot be over emphasized. This is because skill acquisition explains _the usefulness and resourcefulness of man, his environment and natural resources. Skill acquisition is important in the following ways:

  1. It is through acquired skills that people are classified in order of resourcefulness and rewards.
  2. Through skill acquisition people experience improved quality of life.
  3. Through the expression of skills human capabilities are appreciated.
  4. Skill acquisition result in enhanced economic and social development.
  5. Owning to the importance of skill acquisition, governments and corporate institutions have built and established training centers.

 

Unit 5

 How Skills can be Acquired

There are various ways of learning skills. This is because some skills have peculiar ways they can be taught and learned. These include.

  1. Skills acquisition by demonstration and practice.
  2. Skill can be learnt by mere or intensive observation.
  3. Some skills can be learnt by both theory i.e explanation of ideas practical lessons.
  4. Some skills can be learned through programmed apprenticeship or internship and through vocational technical education.
  5. Some can be learnt through seminars and workshops.
  6. Some skills can be learnt by identification with experts or skill masters. That is, one can learn a skill by close association with the skill-master accompanied by a kin interest in what he does.
  7. Some skills can be learnt by questions prolonged and intense study, research and scholarship. This means by reading books, receiving esoteric teachings and instructions over a long period of time.
  8. Some skills can be learned through habit over time.
