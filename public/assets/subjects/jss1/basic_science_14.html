Unit 1

The Meaning of Ethics

 Ethics is a practical science which studies the rightness and fairness, wrongness and unfairness of human behaviour and activities. It is a science which tries to direct on how actions can be right, fair, generally useful to all and beneficial for good life or good living. In other words, ethics passes judgment on actions and behaviour of man showing whether they are good, just, right, fair, beneficial or bad, unjust, wrong, unfair or even harmful to man and his environment.

We can define ethics as a normative Science of the conduct of human beings living in societies. A science which judges the rightness and wrongness of conduct or the goodness or badness of action. Thus, ethics is the science of conduct or science of morals concerned with human character and conducts. It is a code of moral principles that govern or influence a person's behaviour. Ethics is generally referred to as moral philosophy. It is not only a guide to human behaviour and actions but also a guide to ideas, rules, laws, norms, customs, traditions, scientific theories, application and technologies. Nothing escapes its judgment. Its major objective is to ensure that actions and behaviour are rightly done. It protects individuals from - physical, psychological, spiritual and personal harm as a result of other people's behaviour, actions, ideas or application of technologies.

 

Unit 2

Right Wrong Application of Science

Science can be said to be an investigation or inquiry of or about things using experiments and observation. Scientific activities have three major components namely scientific attitudes, scientific processes and scientific products.

  1. Scientific Attitudes: scientific attitudes are concerned with values beliefs and scientific behaviours investigation involving.
  2. Suspending judgment until Sufficient data about the problem have been collected.
  3. Curiosity about things or events
  4. Expecting failure where possible
  5. Being open-minded.
  6. Approaching issues/things objectively i.e without bias
  7. Scientific Processes: It establishes ways of investigating problems. These processes include:
  • Observation
  • Planning or designing the experiment
  • Experimenting
  • Prediction
  • Measuring
  • Classifying
  • Collection
  • Analysis or evaluation of data
  • Asking insightful questions
  • Inferring or drawing conclusion from data collected and analysed
  • Building up concepts, principles, collected and analysis made

 

 

  1. Scientific Products: Scientific Products are developed from the data, collected and analyzed. The analysis produces these products:
  • Concepts which explain ideas specifically or generally
  • Scientific principles which explain relationship of things e.g if water is exposed to the boiling point by heat, it produces vapour.
  • Theory which is a_ group of principles explaining a_ natural phenomenon and _ predicting its behaviour.
  • Laws which are universal truths established as a result of experimental findings. Generally, these scientific components can rightly or wrongly be applied. While trying to solve the society's problems and while trying to apply scientific attitudes, processes and products on human and environmental problems, care must be taken not to negatively injure the same life of the individual that we are trying to preserve or protect. Certain scientific activities which appeal superficially solvent and beneficial could have a long term or endemic negative consequences on human, non-human and environmental existence. A workable scientific method given the same conditions can be replicated anywhere any time without considering age, tribe or status. This is what is known as reliability of a true science. Any right scientific theory has universal principles which can be replicated under the same circumstances. There are basically two ways a verified and proven scientific and technological device can be applied while attempting to solve human, social and environmental problems. These two ways science and technology can be applied are: right application and wrong application.
