There are basically four elementary skills in the game of hockey. They include the following:

Explanation of the main skills

 

  1.  Hit This means striking the ball with the flat surface of the stick. To make a good distance in this skill, the hockey stick is gripped with both hands on the handle in astride position. The stick is thrown backwards but not above the Shoulder level. in a split second the Stick is lowered to hit the ball on the ground to where it is expected to go.
  2.  The Stop This means banging a moving or travelling ball to a halt using the flat Surface of the hockey stick. Usually the right hand is lowered towards the neck of the stick for effective Stopping.
  3. Dribbling

This is moving the ball with short " ‘pushes using the stick, into the opponent's half of the field Sometimes long pushes are necessary provided they are within the control of the player.

 

 

  1. Passing:

This involves striking or pushing the ball to a teammate for the purpose of gaining advantage over the opponent. Passes could be short or long depending on the position of the teammate.
