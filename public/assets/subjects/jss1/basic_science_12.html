RADIOACTIVITY

 

Topic: Radioactivity Introduction

 

In 1896, a French scientist called Henry Becquerel, discovered that the element Uranium emitted radiations which blackened a photographic plate and like x-rays, could penetrate through paper and similar opaque ‘materials. The radiation was spontaneous and was being emitted all the time, apparently without anything causing it. These radiations are alpha particle, beta-particle and gamma radiation.

 

Unit 1

Radioactive Elements

 

These are those substances that spontaneously emit particles and radiations from their nucleus as a result of the instability of the nuclei of the atom. Example; radium, radon, polonium and Uranium.

 Types of Radioactivity

 There are basically two types of radioactivity.

  1. Natural radioactivity
  2. Artificial radioactivity
  3. Natural Radioactivity: This is the spontaneous disintegration of an unstable nuclei with the emission of alpha-particle, beta-particle and gamma radiation or a combination of any two or all of the three with the release of energy/heat.

Unit 4

Uses of Radioactivity.

They include the following

  1. Radioactive dating: for determining the age of rock or the time when an animal died.
  2. Radiotherapy: Radioactive sources are used in diagnosing and treatment of diseases.
  3. It can be used in sterilizing medical instrument in clinics and hospitals.
  4. Used in agriculture as radioactive tracers and preservatives.
  5. Used in generation of nuclear power energy
  6. Used in production of nuclear weapons
  7. Used in metal industries to detect flaws in materials.

  

 

Unit 5

Dangers of Radioactive Rays

 

Radioactive sources can be dangerous to the body if one is exposed to the radiation for a long time.

 

  • Cells in tissue of the body can be destroyed
  • Radioactive elements can contaminate water and air
  • Radioactive elements can upset the normal chemical reaction of the body
  • Radioactive elements can cause damage to the bone marrows and also affect the blood
  • Overexposure to radioactive radiation can cause death.
