Topic 5

GROUP/COMBINED EVENTS

Topic: Group/Combined Events

Unit 1

 Classification

  1. Pentathlon: This is a sport that involves five sporting activities, for instance;

 

  • Standing jump
  • 100m dash
  • Discus
  • Javelin throw
  • 200m dash

 

The first documented pentathlon dates back to 708BC in Ancient Greece. ¢ Men and women participate in Pentathlon. The men's athletic pentathlon has been competed for since 1912 while that of women was introduced in the 2000 Summer Olympics.

Pentathlon may last for one or two days consecutively in this order;

Day 1: 100m dash, shot put and high jump.

Day II: - Long jump and high jump. If the sport will last only a day, there may be two sessions; Morning and Afternoon.

 

 

Unit 2

Decathlon

 It is a sport that involves ten sporting events. Originally it was for men alone but recently women’s decathlon has been recognized internationally.  Decarthlon consists of ten track and field events arranged as follows

  • 4 runs
  • 3 jumps
  • 3 throws

The word decathlon originated from a Greek word “deka” meaning “ten” while “athlon” means “feat” or remarkable achievement.

The competition runs for two Consecutive days because of the number of events involved. It is usually arranged in this order,

 

Day I

100m dash

Discus throw

Pole Vault

Javelin throw

400m dash

Day II

100m hurdles

Long jump

Short put

High Jump

150m

Unit 3

Scoring System

Pentathlon and decathlon have similar scoring system. This is done by using a scoring sheet/table to record the scores of each participant in each event.

 

Unit 4

 Rules and Regulations

Every competition has its rules and regulations. In the case of pentathlon and decathlon, the rules include;

  • Competitors must compete in all the events or you are out.
  • In the case of field events such as jumps and throws, participants are given three chances.
  • In the case of track events a participant is allowed two false starts after which he/she is disqualified.
  • The women are allowed less weighty implements such as shot put and discus.
  • Their own hurdles are lower than that of the men.

 

Facilities and Equipment

These depend on the events involved since the focus of this book is on athletic pentathlon and decathlon; all facilities and equipment used for should be athletics competition involved. They include:

  • Tracks and fields.
  • Starting gun.
  • Jump stands for high jump
  • Foam and pole vault.
  • Shot put.
  • Spike shoes
  • Javelin
  • Hurdle stands.
  • Run-way for long jump and so on.

 

Unit 5

 Safety Rules

Safety measures are very important because many events are involved.

They include:

  • Medical fitness of all competitors should be ascertained.
  • All equipment for the competition must be in good condition.
