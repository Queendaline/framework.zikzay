Personal Health-Ageing

What is age ng?

 

Ageing can simply be defined as the process of getting old or becoming old. When a child is born. he or she passes through many stages in life for instance he passes through:

  • Infancy
  • Toddler
  • Child
  • Puberty
  • Teenager
  • young man/woman
  • Middle age man/woman
  • Old man/woman
  • very old man/woman

Ageing can also be seen as the accumulation of changes that render human beings progressively more inactive. Ageing is not a one-day phenomenon. For instance, an old man complains of headache ‘today, tomorrow it is backache, the following day it is his legs and muscles. That is why a writer defines ageing as a progressive functional decline or gradual deterioration of physiological function with age, including a decrease in fecundity (fertility).

 

Ageing is characterized by changes which include:

 

  • Gradual reduction in height and weight due to loss of muscle and bone mass.
  • Reduction in reaction time.
  • Decline in sexual activities.
  • Lower metabolic rate.
  • Loss of interest in exercises.
  • Decline in hearing and vision i.e. hearing and vision impaired.
  • Deterioration in agility, flexibility, strength endurance, etc.
  • Gradual breakdown or decay in memory (amnesia).
  • Increase in inflammation levels resulting in arthritis.
