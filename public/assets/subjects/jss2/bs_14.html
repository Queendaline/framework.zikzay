TOPIC 14: RECREATION, LEISURE AND DANCE ACTIVITIES

Recreation is a non-competitive physical activity that takes place for the purpose of leisure. It is time spent in an activity one loves to engage in.

Scope of Recreation

Recreation can be classified into two broad headings, namely: indoor and outdoor.

  1. Indoor Recreational Activities: These are activities that divert, amuse or stimulate one’s mind, body and soul, performed inside the house, hall or gymnasium. These indoor activities include Music, dance, watching movies, playing video games, card games, table tennis, reading, writing, craft-making, cooking, painting, badminton and so on.
  2. Outdoor Recreational Activities: These are activities that divert, amuse or stimulate one’s mind, body and soul, performed outside the house, hall or gymnasium. They include swimming, fishing, lawn tennis, cycling, horse riding, traveling, boating, visiting a museum or amusement park, photography, volleyball, basketball, beach games, hiking, and football amongst others.

Brief History of Table Tennis

The origin of table tennis is not clear. Some authorities said it originated in China. Others said it originated in USA, yet others said it started in England. All the same, the game was made popular by the USA. Originally, the game was known as a parlor game. After some years, the rules were standardized and it became a popular sport. It is played in almost every nation of the world today. The equipment in table tennis includes the table, the bat, the net and the ball.

Basic Skills and Techniques

The Grip: Grip in table tennis is very important because a good and proper grip ensures good service and stroke. There are two types of grip; the forehand grip and the backhand grip. In the forehand or handshake grip, the right hand enters into the handle of the bat as if to shake hands with it. The thumb faces the left hand and the index finger, the right. The rest of the fingers curl around the handle of the bat. In the backhand grip, the side gripped by the index finger faces the table.

The Service: The server stands a little distance away from his own area of the table with the ball on his left palm. As soon as his opponent is ready, he tosses the ball up and hits the ball down on his own side of the table before it crosses to the opponent’s side.

Let: This means that the service should be retaken either because the opponent was not ready when the first service was made or the served ball touched the top of the net before touching the opponent’s side of the table.

Strokes: Strokes are of two types: Offensive strokes and defensive strokes. Offensive strokes include the forehand drive, backhand drive and the smash while the defensive strokes include the backhand chop and the forehand chop. Strokes are meant to dislodge an opponent so that he will not be able to return the ball.

Gymnastics and Aerobics

Gymnastics and aerobics make use of creative rhythms in order to bring out the beauty of the sports. The origin of the sport could be traced back to ancient Greece but modern gymnastics is the brainchild of the Germans. Gymnastics refers to activities that are performed in a gymnasium. It is the art of performing various types of physical exercises and uncommon feats of skill. It could be classified in to two broad headings; floor activities and apparatus work.

Floor Activities: Floor activities are gymnastic activities performed on the floor. They are divided into two – stunt and tumbling. Stunt includes exercises such as wheel barrow, frog jump, rabbit jump, duck walk, crab walk, duck fight and cat crawl. Tumbling involves exercises such as forward roll, backward roll, head stand, head spring, hand spring, cat spring and somersault.

Apparatus Work: These are activities performed on apparatus such as parallel bar, beam bar, climbing rope, balance beams and chinning bars. Activities on these apparatus include: Through vault, astride vault, rope climbing, pull up, chin up, gate vault, thief vault and neck spring vault.

Importance of Gymnastics

  • The gymnast enjoys a flexible body
  • He enjoys self confidence
  • His movement is always with grace and poise
  • His agility and alertness are enhanced
  • His economy is improved

Aerobics

Aerobic is a form of physical exercise that combines rhythmic exercise with stretching and strength training routines with the goal of improving all elements of fitness such as flexibility, muscular strength and cardiovascular fitness. This is usually performed to music and may be practiced in a group setting. Another goal of aerobics is to prevent illness and promote physical fitness.

Types of Dance Activities

Dance simply means an art that involves movement of the whole body. It is performed in many cultures of the world as a form of emotional expression, social interaction or exercise. Dance sometimes is use to express idea or tell a story. Dance activities can be classified into singing games, cultural or folk dance and creative dance.

Singing Games: Singing game is a dance where people sing a song and dance according to the beat. An example is fire on the mountain and Kpu Kpu Ogele. It is one of the simplest forms of dance.

Cultural or Folk Dances: These are traditional dances found in a particular culture or tribe or group. They are contemporary dances of the past and present of a people.

Creative Dance: Creative dances are movements people make to communicate their feeling about certain things and ideas. Creative dances can only be possible when a child has sufficient movement skill and vocabulary.
