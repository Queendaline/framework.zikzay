TOPIC 13: Human Trafficking

Human trafficking is the sale, transport and profit from human beings who are forced to work for others. This business is tantamount to slavery. Against their will, millions of people around the world are forced to work for the profit of others. Trafficking in persons is a serious crime and gross violation of human rights. Every year, thousands of men women and children fall into the hand of traffickers in their own countries and abroad. These unfortunate human beings are subjected to prostitution, begging, working in sweatshops and other forms of dehumanizing activities.

How Does Trafficking Happen

Young women are usually fascinated when they are promised a good job with a fat pay packet. In most cases, they use force, threat, coercion, abduction, fraud and deception. On arrival at their destination, their passports, visas and return tickets are taken from them and they are forced to do any manner of work they are asked to do. Even the money they earn is not given to them.

Causes of Trafficking

The major causes of trafficking include:

  1. Poverty: Some people around the world are living in abject poverty to the extent that they cannot boast of a good meal a day. This could be caused by poor family planning on the part of the parents, the death of one or both of the parents, crime, diseases, famine, war and natural disasters
  2. Some People Derive Pleasure in Exploitation: Women and children are ready markets for traffickers
  3. Lack of contentment
  4. Laziness
  5. Easy money on the part of the traffickers and their victims

Health Implication of Trafficking

The act of trafficking and the attendant human right violations can have very serious consequences for the victim.

  • Women who are subjected to these inhuman treatments can suffer from physical and mental problems such as physical abuse, exhaustion and starvation
  • They can suffer from severe injuries such as broken bones, bruises or burns
  • At times, they contract sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) including HIV/AIDS
  • Sometimes they suffer severe stress and depression
  • Those of them in the sex trade face the risk of unwanted pregnancy and miscarriages thereby endangering their health
  • They also suffer from post-traumatic stress disorder and anxiety. In extreme cases may lead to self-mutation or even suicide
  • Some have even gone mad

Solution to Human Trafficking

From all indications, the key countries where this illicit trade is going on are paying lip service to the whole saga. They know what to do.

  • Slave trade/human trafficking should be criminalized
  • Wars should be avoided and poverty should be tackled
  • People should be contented with what they have. This way, they will not fall victim to scams
  • Good education will help
