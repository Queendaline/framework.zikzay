TOPIC 16: FIRST AID SAFETY EDUCATION

An accident is a situation in which someone is injured or something is damaged without anyone intending it to be so. An accident is an unplanned mishap or an unforeseen occurrence. Accident can occur anytime and anywhere. It could happen on the road, in the home, on the playground, in the office, in the classroom and so on. Injury is a result of accident.

Injury could be seen as a wound or damage to the body as a result of accident. Injury could be classified as: Multiple injury, serious injury or minor injury.

Safety Measures

Safety Measures at Home: it is said that accidents are unplanned mishaps but they can be prevented. The under-listed safety measures are panacea to home accidents. They include:

  • Keeping all drugs and medicines away from the reach of children and the mentally unstable
  • The floor must always be dry because wet floors are always slippery
  • Sharp objects like needles, nails, knives and so on should be kept away from children
  • Electrical appliances must be switched off as soon as light go off when not in use
  • Gas cookers must be turned off as soon as cooking is done with
  • When using gas cookers, always be at alert to know when there is gas leakage
  • Always ensure there is good light at night to avoid tripping

Safety Measures on the Playground: Physical education by its nature is prone to accidents because a lot of activities vital to the normal development of the child are involved. The physical education teacher should not, as a result of fear of injury, deny the children these programs. The only thing the teacher should do is to be safety conscious. These include:

  • Playground and gymnasium should be in good condition. Defective equipment should be repaired or replaced immediately
  • Competent supervisors and instructors should be employed to handle the students
  • There should be medical examination to ascertain the student’s level of fitness before participation
  • Jumping into physical activity without adequate warm up can be dangerous. So warm up is important to free the muscles, reduce friction in joints and help reduce injury in sports.
  • The interest, physical and emotional development of the young people must be considered
  • The child should be exposed to activities appropriate to their skill.

Safety Measures on the Road: The different categories of people that use the road are the motorists, the pedestrian, the cyclist, the motor cyclist, and the traffic wardens. The following are the safety measure each of the following categories of road users should adhere to.

  • They should strictly obey traffic rules
  • Seatbelts should be fastened at all times
  • Drivers should never drive after consuming alcoholic drinks or under the influence of drugs
  • A rest of at least 15 minutes should be observed after driving for 4 hours while on a long journey
  • The driver should avoid fatigue
  • The pedestrian should always walk on the left hand side of the road facing on-coming vehicles
  • The pedestrian should put on bright colored shirts while walking on along the road at night
  • The pedestrian should look left, right and left again before crossing the road where zebra crossing and overhead bridges are not available
  • Cyclists shouldn’t carry anything that will affect their balance
  • Motorcyclists should always wear a safety helmet
  • The speed limit should always be observed
  • A vehicle in bad condition should not be on the road
  • Children should be strapped at the back seat of a car
  • Children should not be carried on the lap while driving
  • Children should not be allowed to fiddle with the door of the car while in motion.

Sports Injuries, Causes and First Aid Treatment

Sports injuries are injuries sustained in the field of play, playground or any other place where physical activities are performed. Sports injuries include the following:

Sprain: A sprain is an injury to a joint in which one or more ligaments that support the joint are over-stretched or even torn. Sprain is usually caused by violent twist of the joint while in motion. Parts of the body that are commonly sprained include the knee, ankle, wrist and fingers. Signs of sprain are severe pain, swelling at the joint, tender to touch, black and blue discoloration and difficulty in using that joint.

First Aid Treatment for Sprain: The first aider should do the following:

  • Immobilize the sprained part of the body using the bandage carefully so as not to also suffocate blood circulation
  • Apply cold compress to reduce pain and swelling
  • The victim must not use the sprained part until the doctor allows it
  • The injured part should be in a sling.

Strain: Strain is an injury in the muscle or any other part of the body due to over use. This is especially caused by lack of warm-up exercise. Signs of strain include muscle tear, muscle stiffness and pain.

 

First Aid Treatment for Strain: The first aider should do the following:

  • Rest the strained part in a comfortable bed
  • Apply cold compress
  • Strap the affected region
  • Apply hot compress after some time.

Dislocation: This is a displacement of one of the bones that form a joint. In dislocation, the ligaments and fibrous capsule that hold the joint are over-stretched and torn. This could be caused by a blow on the affected part. Some of the signs of dislocation are difficulty in movement or no movement at all, excruciating pain and swelling of the affected region.

First Aid Treatment for Dislocation: The first aider should do the following:

  • Immobilize the affected part of the body
  • Apply cold compress on the affected part
  • Do not try to push it into place
  • Send the victim to a doctor.

Fracture: Fracture is a break in the bone. Fracture can happen in any part of the body where there are bones. It could be caused by a fall, automobile accident and so on. There are three major types of fracture namely: Greenstick fracture, simple fracture and compound fracture. There could be multiple fractures in which case a particular bone is broken in more than one place. Some of the signs of fracture are the inability to use the fractured part, discoloration on the affected part, pains, swelling, tenderness at the fractured part and the broken can be felt.

First Aid Treatment for Fracture: The first aider should do the following:

  • Immobilize the fractured part
  • Apply cold compress if the skin is not badly torn
  • Control bleeding if the skin is torn and blood is gushing out
  • Reassure the victim
  • Send the victim to the doctor

Bruise: Bruises are injuries inside the body tissue; the skin may not be torn. In this case, the small blood vessels are broken in the bruised tissues and blood escapes into these tissues causing it to swell and become dark. It could be caused by a blow from a hard object. Some of the signs of bruises include swelling, pain and black discoloration as the affected area heals.

First Aid Treatment for Bruise: The first aider should do the following:

  • Apply cold compress
  • If the hand or feet is bruised, dip repeatedly in ice water for a few minutes and remove
  • Put the bruised part in a sling to rest it
  • The following day apply hot and cold compress for fast healing.

Wounds: Wounds consist of a break in the skin which affects the tissues and muscles and may or may not affect the bone. Wounds may be closed or open. A wound is closed when there is no visible break on the skin. It is an open wound when there is visible break on the skin and blood is gushing out. There are different types of wounds as follows:

  1. Abrasion: This is caused by friction between a hard and rough surface and the skin resulting to the removal of the first layer of the skin
  2. Lacerated Wound: this is caused by a rough and hard object leaving a jagged cut on the skin
  3. Punctured Wound: It is a puncture of the skin by a sharp pointed object like needles, pins and so on.
  4. Incised Wound: This type of wound is usually caused by sharp objects such as razor blade and knives causing blood to gush out.

First Aid Treatment for Wounds: The first aider should do the following:

  • Arrest bleeding using direct pressure
  • Control pressure points nearest to the wound to reduce flow of blood to the site
  • Wash wound with sterile water and soap
  • Wash the skin around the mouth of the wound
  • Use clean water to flush out any dirt inside the wound
  • The first aider should not administer any medicine to the victim
  • Take the victim to the doctor
