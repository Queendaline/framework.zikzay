TOPIC 18: ROAD ACCIDENTS

It has been observed that road accidents have claimed more lives than all diseases put together. There are three main causes of accidents namely: The human factor, mechanical factor and environmental factor.

The Human Factor: Some of the human factors that cause road accidents are:

  • Over speeding
  • Driving when tired
  • Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Driving a faulty vehicle
  • Dangerous overtaking
  • Entertaining distractions while driving

Mechanical Factor: These are factors that cause road accident as a result of the poor state of the vehicle. Some of these include tire burst, brake failure, poor steering power, incessant engine problem, smoke from exhaust pipe hindering the vision of the driver, poor lighting system, Absence of rear mirror and non-functional horn.

Environmental Factor: Environmental factors entail everything around the vehicle. They include things such as:

  • Potholes along the road
  • Meandering road
  • Absence of road markings
  • Heavy rain and poor drainage systems
  • Slippery surface as a result of spillage on the road
  • Debris or blockages on the road
  • Absence of road signs

First Aid: When a road accident occurs, it is important to know what to do to save life that will otherwise have been lost. A good first aider should do the following:

  • Remove casualties from danger; divert on-coming vehicles from the accident scene.
  • Check to find out the victims that are more serious than the others in an accident scene
  • In case of cessation of breathing, the first aider should perform mouth-to-mouth respiration
  • If the victim is bleeding profusely, locate pressure points closest to the site of injury and press it in and apply cold compress
  • All casualties should be taken to the hospital first before those confirmed dead.
