TOPIC 9: MATERIALS AND THEIR COMMON USES

Materials commonly used in technology include wood, metals, ceramics, plastics and rubber. We will be focusing our attention on the uses of these technological materials.

Wood: Uses of Wood

Wood is used today in the following industries and processes.

  • Furniture: The furniture found in our homes such as beds, cupboards, tables, chairs, wardrobes and so on are made of wood
  • Building Construction Industry: In building industry, the door frames, doors, windows, wall plates, stair cases, beams, roofing rafters and so on require the use of wood
  • Education: Exercise books, textbooks, magazines, pencils and so on are items made of wood
  • Tool Making: The handles of mallets, hammers, screw drivers, chisels, files, shovels, spades, axes machetes and so on are made of wood
  • Bridges and Poles: Most farm bridges in rural areas, electric poles, railway sleepers and so on are made of wood
  • Transport: The lorry bodies, railway coaches and wagons are made of wood
  • Clothing: Some soles of shoes and most heels of shoes are made of wood
  • Packaging: Items used for packaging products are made of wood

Reasons for Widespread Use of Wood

The following reasons account for the widespread use of wood for numerous purposes.

  • Wood is easily obtained
  • Unfinished wood (lumber) is comparatively cheap
  • Wood is easily worked with tools
  • When compared to other materials of wide availability, wood possesses considerable strength for its relatively low weight
  • Wood possesses good acoustic properties. Thus, it is widely employed in the manufacture of musical instruments
  • Weather and climatic changes do not seriously affect wood that has been properly finished
  • Wood can absorb fire retardants. This makes wood so treated to be fire-resistant and hence renders it more useful in engineering
  • Wood is an excellent non-conductor of heat. Therefore, it is widely employed in wall paneling to make the interior of dwelling cool in hot weather and warm in cold weather
  • Wood does not quickly deteriorate in value if properly handled and protected

Metal: Uses of Ferrous and Non-Ferrous Materials

Ferrous Materials

Metals

Uses

Cast Iron

For making machine tool beds, vice bodies, cylinder blocks, surface table and plate piston rings

Mild Steel

For making bolts and nuts, tin plate, tube and, non-cutting tools

High Carbon Steel

For making hammer heads, springs, cutting tools, pliers, screw drivers, knives, scribes, taps, files, hacksaw, chisel and hacksaw blade

Rough Iron

For making chairs, boiler, plates, haulage gears, crane hooks and so on

 

Non-Ferrous Metals

Metal

Uses

Copper

Used for making soldering bits, water pipes, wires, electrical conductors, roofing, base for many alloys, boxes, rivets and bowls for decorative purposes

Lead

For manufacture of plates in car batteries, sheeting for roofing, water piping, base for many alloys

Tin

Mainly used as the base for many alloys like bronze and as protective coating on copper wire and tin plate sheets

Aluminum

For making electric cable, air craft parts, aluminum foils, engine parts, tubes, cooking utensils and used as a base for aluminum alloy

Zinc

For making roofing sheets, galvanizing protective coating on sheet and also used as a base for many alloys such as brass

 

Non-Ferrous Alloys

Alloy

Uses

Solder

For joining most non-ferrous materials

Brass

Used for making rivets, rods, wires, screws, sheets, castings, decorations, ammunitions and so on

Bell Metal

Mainly used for making bells

Gum Metal

Used for making pump bodies, steam pipe fittings, gears and so on

Aluminum-Bronze

Used for electrical and sewage works, making under-water fittings, also used in paper making industry and for architectural works

Cartridge Brass

Used for making stampings and pressings and for drawing sections

Phosphor-Bronze

Mainly used for making springs and bearings

 

Ceramics: Ceramics and uses

Ceramics

Uses

Shiny Exterior

Used for decorations such as flower vases, tiles, plates and for glass ware sculpture

Corrosion Resistant

Used for storing water, chemicals. Making sinks, water closets and so on

Heat Resistant

Used for making furnaces, bakery oven, refractory and household utensils

Electrical Insulation

Used for making electrical accessories such as plugs, switches, lamp holders and so on

High compressive strength

Used for construction of buildings and other structures e.g. for support of storage tanks and so on

 

Rubber: Uses of Rubber

Rubber is used for the following.

  • Rain coats and boots
  • Vehicle tubes and tyres
  • Car bumpers
  • Gloves
  • Rubber shoes, slippers and sandals
  • Used as an electrical insulator.

Plastics: Uses of Plastics

  • Plastic materials are cheap and light. They are used to manufacture buckets, jerry cans, plastic bottles tanks, cups, plates and mathematical instruments found in a mathematical set
  • Used in building for insulation and ceiling decoration
  • Used in fan blades and electrical insulation
  • Used to manufacture tubes and pipes as well as pen, toys, toothbrushes, battery cases and so on.
